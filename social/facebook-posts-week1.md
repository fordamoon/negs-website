## Friday, April 11 — The Tip

**Voice:** NEGS (brand)
**Type:** Educational / actionable

Here's something most tourism operators can do this weekend that will save them money this summer.

Open your booking records from last season. Open your marketing invoices. Put them side by side, month by month.

Look for the months where you spent more but bookings didn't go up. That's a channel (or channels) that isn't pulling its weight.

Now look for the months where bookings surged without a spending increase. That's organic momentum — word of mouth, repeat guests, SEO. Those are the channels worth investing in.

This isn't analytics. It's accounting. But almost nobody does it because the data lives in five different places and nobody has time to pull it together during the season.

Do it now. Before May hits. Before you're too busy to think about it.

## Saturday, April 12 — The Myth

**Voice:** Matthew (personal)
**Type:** Opinion / educational

Hot take: most tourism businesses should be spending less on Facebook ads and more on email.

I know. Facebook is where you have followers. You boost a post, you see likes, it feels like it's working.

But here's what I keep seeing in the data: email drives 3–5x more booking revenue per dollar than Facebook for repeat guests. And repeat guests are the backbone of most seasonal businesses.

Facebook is solid for awareness — it's how new people find you. But for converting someone who stayed with you last summer into a booking for this summer? That's email. A simple "we're opening reservations" message to your past guest list will outperform a month of boosted posts.

You don't need Mailchimp. You don't need a marketing automation platform. You need a list and a send button.

If you're not emailing your past customers, start this week. It's the highest-ROI thing most tourism businesses aren't doing.
