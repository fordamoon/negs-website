# Facebook Business Page Setup Checklist

**New England Growth Studio**
**Last updated:** March 30, 2026

---

## 1. Create the Page

- Go to facebook.com/pages/create
- Choose category: **Marketing Consultant** (also add "Business Consultant" and "Internet Marketing Service" as secondary categories)
- Page name: **New England Growth Studio**
- Don't use "NEGS" as the page name — spell it out for search discoverability

---

## 2. Username / Vanity URL

- Set username to: **@newenglandgrowthstudio**
- If taken, try: **@NEGrowthStudio**
- This gives you facebook.com/newenglandgrowthstudio — clean for print, cards, and links

---

## 3. Profile Picture

- **Use the NEGS wordmark** on dark background (#070D19)
- Dimensions: **170 x 170 px** (displays at 176x176 on desktop, 36x36 in comments)
- Keep it simple — the wordmark with "New England" in white and "Growth Studio" in neon green (#39FF14) on the dark navy background
- No text-heavy logos — this renders tiny in the feed
- Export as PNG with transparent or dark background

---

## 4. Cover Photo

- Dimensions: **820 x 312 px** (displays at 820x312 on desktop, crops to 640x360 on mobile)
- Use a version of the LinkedIn cover adapted to this ratio — lighthouse illustration on left, "Systems over chaos. / Execution over noise." on right
- Keep critical content in the center 640px — edges get cropped on mobile
- Dark navy background (#070D19) with neon green (#39FF14) illustration, white text
- No phone numbers or URLs in the cover — Facebook deprioritizes pages that do this

---

## 5. About Section

Fill in every field. Facebook uses these for search ranking.

**Short Description (155 characters):**
> AI-powered growth consulting for tourism & hospitality businesses across New England. Stop guessing. Start adapting. Keep growing.

**Long Description:**
> New England Growth Studio helps tourism operators, hospitality businesses, marinas, boat rentals, and boutique inns across New Hampshire, Maine, Vermont, and Massachusetts grow with data — not guesswork.
>
> We build tools and provide consulting that connects your marketing spend to actual bookings. Our flagship product, Headwaters, is a marketing attribution tool built specifically for seasonal tourism businesses. Upload your data, get a clear answer in 60 seconds.
>
> Founded by Matthew Alighieri. Based in New Hampshire's Lakes Region.

**Additional Info:**
- Website: **https://newenglandgrowthstudio.com**
- Email: **matthewalighieri@gmail.com** (or a dedicated business email when ready)
- Phone: Add if you want calls, skip if you don't — it's optional
- Location: **New Hampshire** (you can set a service area without a street address)
- Hours: Set to **Responds within a few hours** rather than specific business hours — you're a solo operation and don't want to imply 9-5 availability
- Founded: **2025**
- Price range: **$$** (signals professional services, not budget or luxury)

---

## 6. Service Area

Facebook lets you set a service area radius. Set it to cover:

- New Hampshire (primary)
- Maine
- Vermont
- Massachusetts

This helps you appear in local searches without listing a street address.

---

## 7. Call-to-Action Button

- Set the CTA button to: **Learn More**
- Link it to: **https://newenglandgrowthstudio.com**
- Alternatives if you want to drive a specific action later: "Sign Up" (link to Headwaters), "Send Message" (opens Messenger), or "Contact Us"
- "Learn More" is the safest default — it's low-friction and works for all visitor types

---

## 8. Page Tabs

Reorder and clean up the default tabs. Remove clutter.

**Keep:**
- Home
- About
- Posts
- Reviews (enable these — social proof matters for service businesses)
- Photos/Videos

**Remove or hide:**
- Shop
- Events (unless you plan to host webinars or local meetups)
- Groups
- Offers

Go to Settings > Templates and Tabs to reorder.

---

## 9. Messaging Settings

- Turn on Messenger for the page
- Set up an **Instant Reply** (auto-response when someone messages):

> Thanks for reaching out. I usually respond within a few hours. If it's about Headwaters or marketing attribution, feel free to share a bit about your business and I'll get back to you with something useful. — Matthew

- Turn on "Show response time" — it builds trust
- Consider setting up a simple FAQ auto-response for common questions

---

## 10. Page Settings & Permissions

- **Page Roles:** Just you for now. Add a backup admin (trusted person) in case you get locked out — this is the #1 Facebook page disaster
- **Visitor Posts:** Allow visitors to post on your page. You can moderate before they appear
- **Profanity Filter:** Turn on (Medium setting)
- **Country/Age Restrictions:** None needed
- **Content Distribution:** Make sure "Allow others to share your posts" is ON

---

## 11. Linked Accounts

- **Instagram:** If you have or plan an Instagram account, link it through Meta Business Suite so you can cross-post
- **WhatsApp:** Optional — link if you use WhatsApp for business communication
- **LinkedIn:** No native link, but post the same content on both with platform-specific tweaks (the content calendar already accounts for this)

---

## 12. Meta Business Suite Setup

- Go to **business.facebook.com** and claim your page under a Business Account
- This gives you access to: scheduled posting, inbox management, basic analytics, and ad tools (for later)
- Even if you're not running ads now, having the business account set up means you can boost posts or run targeted campaigns when you're ready
- Install the **Meta Business Suite mobile app** — it's the fastest way to respond to messages and check notifications

---

## 13. Facebook Pixel (Do This Now)

Even if you're not running ads yet, install the pixel on newenglandgrowthstudio.com:

- Go to Meta Business Suite > Events Manager > Connect Data Sources > Web > Facebook Pixel
- Name it "NEGS Website Pixel"
- Add the base code to the `<head>` of your site
- This starts collecting visitor data immediately — when you do run ads later, you'll have an audience to retarget instead of starting from zero

---

## 14. First Three Posts (Before Going Live)

Don't launch an empty page. Have at least three posts published before you share the page anywhere:

1. **Intro post** — Who is NEGS, what do you do, who is it for. Keep it short. Use the brand voice. End with "Follow along for marketing insights built for tourism and hospitality operators."

2. **Value post** — Pick one of the educational posts from the content calendar (Post 1 or Post 4 work well). Adapt it slightly for Facebook tone (shorter paragraphs, more conversational).

3. **Headwaters post** — Introduce the product. Use Post 2 from the content calendar.

This gives visitors something to read and signals that the page is active.

---

## 15. Review Settings

- Go to Settings > Templates and Tabs > Reviews and enable them
- Reviews are critical for service businesses — they show up in search and build trust
- After your first few client interactions, ask satisfied clients to leave a review
- Respond to every review (positive or negative) personally as Matthew, not as "the team"

---

## 16. Content Publishing Rhythm

Once the page is live, follow the content calendar:

- **3 posts per week** (Tuesday, Thursday, Sunday)
- Use Meta Business Suite to schedule posts in advance — batch a week's worth on Monday
- Cross-post to Instagram Stories when relevant (behind-the-scenes content works well there)
- Respond to every comment within 24 hours — Facebook's algorithm rewards engagement velocity

---

## 17. Quick Wins After Launch

**Week 1:**
- Invite personal Facebook friends who are in tourism/hospitality to like the page
- Share the page from your personal profile with a short note: "Built something for tourism operators in New England. If you know anyone in the industry, I'd appreciate a share."
- Post the page link on your LinkedIn profile

**Week 2:**
- Join 3-5 Facebook Groups related to New England tourism, hospitality, or small business (NH Business Network, Maine Tourism Operators, Lakes Region Business, etc.)
- Don't spam your page link — answer questions, provide value, and let your profile (which links to your page) do the work

**Week 3:**
- If you have any client results or testimonials, post them
- Start engaging with other tourism business pages — comment on their posts, share relevant content
