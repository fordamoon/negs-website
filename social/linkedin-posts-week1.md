## Wednesday, April 9 — The Problem

**Voice:** NEGS (brand)
**Type:** Educational

The average tourism operator we talk to is spending between $3,000 and $6,000 a month on marketing. When we ask which channel drives the most bookings, the answer is almost always the same: they don't know.

This isn't a failure of intelligence. It's a failure of infrastructure. Every ad platform reports its own metrics in its own way. Google says one thing. Facebook says another. TripAdvisor takes credit for whatever it can. None of them show you the full picture.

So operators do the only rational thing — they fund everything equally and hope the season is strong enough to cover the waste.

That's an expensive way to run a business. And it's fixable.

The first step is simple: pull your spend by channel for last season, line it up against your booking numbers month by month, and look for the disconnect. Where did spend go up but bookings didn't follow?

That's where the money is leaking.

## Thursday, April 10 — The Case Study

**Voice:** Matthew (personal)
**Type:** Story / proof

Last summer I sat down with an inn owner in the Lakes Region who was spending $4,400 a month across Google, Facebook, and TripAdvisor.

She was splitting it roughly equally because she'd always done it that way.

I ran her numbers through Headwaters — our marketing attribution tool. Took about 45 seconds.

Google was driving 71% of her booking revenue. TripAdvisor: 19%. Facebook: 4%.

She'd been putting a third of her budget into a channel that was driving 4% of her bookings.

She cut Facebook by 75%, moved the money into search and email, and finished the season with the same occupancy rate — spending $1,200 less per month.

That's $14,400 back in one year.

She didn't need a new marketing strategy. She needed one answer to one question: what's actually working?

If you're making budget decisions without that answer, you're guessing. And guessing gets expensive over twelve months.

## Saturday, April 12 — The Insight

**Voice:** Matthew (personal)
**Type:** Educational / opinion

Something I keep seeing: tourism operators spend months agonizing over their marketing budget and then set it on autopilot for the entire season.

Same channels. Same creative. Same monthly spend. January through October.

Meanwhile the businesses that consistently grow are doing something different — they're checking mid-season. They're looking at the data in June and asking "is this still working?" And when something isn't, they move the money.

You don't need to be a data scientist to do this. You need two things: a way to see which channels are driving bookings (that's what Headwaters does), and the willingness to kill something that isn't working even if you've been running it for three years.

The hardest part isn't the analysis. It's letting go of the channel you've always used because it feels safe.

But safe and effective aren't the same thing. And your budget can't afford to fund both.
