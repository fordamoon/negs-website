## What Headwaters Is

Headwaters is a marketing attribution tool built for small tourism and hospitality businesses. It answers one question: **which of your marketing channels is actually driving bookings?**

Not clicks. Not impressions. Not what Facebook tells you or what Google claims. Actual bookings tied to actual spend.

It uses a technique called marketing mix modeling — the same math that Procter & Gamble and Unilever have used for decades to figure out where their ad dollars work. That kind of analysis used to cost $50K+ and require a data team. Headwaters does it for $297/year with a spreadsheet upload and 60 seconds of processing time.

## How It Works — Step by Step

**Step 1: Gather your data.**
You need two things: your marketing spend by channel (Google, Facebook, TripAdvisor, email, print, whatever you use) and your booking numbers. Both broken down by month or by week. Six months minimum. Twelve months is better.

**Step 2: Upload a CSV.**
Headwaters has a simple upload screen. You drop in a spreadsheet with columns for date, spend per channel, and bookings/sales. There's a downloadable template so you know exactly how to format it.

**Step 3: Run the model.**
Hit one button. Headwaters runs a marketing mix model — it applies something called adstock modeling (which accounts for the fact that an ad you ran this week might drive a booking two weeks from now) and then calculates which channels statistically drove revenue.

**Step 4: Read your report.**
You get back a plain-language breakdown showing:
- Which channels drove the most booking revenue (as a percentage)
- A chart comparing what the model predicts vs. what actually happened (the closer the match, the more you can trust the results)
- An R-squared score — think of this as a "confidence rating" for how well the model fits your data
- Individual channel scores showing the strength of each channel's contribution

**That's it.** No dashboards to configure. No integrations. No monthly calls with a consultant. Upload, run, read.

## Common Objections and How to Handle Them

**"I don't have the data."**
You probably do. If you've been running Google Ads, Facebook Ads, or paying for a TripAdvisor listing, those platforms track your spend. Pull the last 12 months. Match it against your booking records. That's the spreadsheet.

**"My business is too small for this."**
If you're spending $2K+ a month on marketing, you're big enough. The question isn't size — it's whether you know which half of your budget is working.

**"I already know what works."**
Maybe. But every operator I've met who said that was surprised by at least one thing in the data. The point isn't to tell you something you don't know — it's to confirm what you think you know and catch the things you're wrong about.

**"This sounds like it's for big companies."**
Marketing mix modeling was for big companies. Headwaters is the version that works with a spreadsheet and costs less than a single day of TripAdvisor premium.

**"I'm not a data person."**
You don't need to be. Upload a file. Read a chart. The output tells you "this channel drove X% of your revenue." That's it.
